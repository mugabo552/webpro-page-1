About KIGALI

Learn about the history, culture, and people of KIGALI

History

Kigali, the capital city of Rwanda, was established in 1907 by German colonial authorities. It became a key administrative center under Belgian rule after World War I. In 1962, when Rwanda gained independence, Kigali was chosen as the national capital due to its central location.

The city has since grown rapidly and played a major role during the 1994 Genocide against the Tutsi. Today, Kigali is known for its cleanliness, safety, and fast development, serving as Rwanda’s economic and political hub.

Geography

Kigali is located in the center of Rwanda, making it easily accessible from all parts of the country. It sits on a series of rolling hills at an average elevation of about 1,567 meters (5,141 feet) above sea level.

The city is surrounded by lush green valleys and small rivers, with a temperate tropical highland climate—mild temperatures and two rainy seasons. Its landscape gives it a scenic beauty and contributes to its name, the “land of a thousand hills.”

Kigali City is divided into 3 districts (sectors):

  1. Gasabo
  2. Kicukiro
  3. Nyarugenge

Each district is further divided into:

  1. Sectors (Imirenge)
  2. Cells (Utugari)
  3. Villages (Imidugudu)

Here’s a quick breakdown:

  1. Gasabo: 15 sectors, 73 cells, 486 villages
  2. Kicukiro: 10 sectors, 41 cells, 333 villages
  3. Nyarugenge: 10 sectors, 47 cells, 360 villag

Culture

Kigali’s culture blends tradition and modern life. It features Rwandan music, dance, art, and community practices like *umuganda*. Kinyarwanda is widely spoken, and the city hosts cultural sites like the Genocide Memorial and art centers.

Economy

Kigali’s economy is the heart of Rwanda’s financial and business activities. It is driven by services, trade, construction, and tourism. The city has a growing tech and innovation sector, and it's known for being one of Africa’s cleanest and fastest-developing capitals.
